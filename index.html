1. Executive Summary

MiniSplitsForLess is a top-tier online HVAC systems seller in the USA, focusing on mini split solutions: single-zone and multi-zone, as well as ductless and ducted installation types. In 2026, our goal is to solidify leadership and become a nationally recognized brand, so customers search for and buy "from us" rather than simply "wherever is cheapest in search".

What We're Already Strong At (building on this)

  • Strong visibility in Google / Shopping, high Merchant Center trust level, and rich snippets (reviews, images, Q&A, videos)
  • High customer rating and trust (social proof)
  • Wide assortment: 6K–60K BTU capacities, single/multi-zone, popular unit types, focus on R454B and Hyper Heat
  • Brands and series that can be positioned as "good / better / best" (Cooper & Hunter, Olivia, Bravo, Olmo, Armbridge)
  • Strong Local/GBP foundation (calls/clicks/actions) that supports trust and brand

Main Goal for 2026

Transform from "highly effective eCommerce" to a nationally recognized mini split systems brand

Clear Priority List (for the entire year)

  • Brand Demand: grow branded queries and direct traffic (recognition + trust)
  • Search & Shopping: scale sales without losing margin (campaign structure + assortment control)
  • Email/CRM: grow revenue share (through automation and segments, no spam)
  • SEO + GEO: dominate informational intent and AI answers (AI Overviews, Copilot, Perplexity, etc.)
  • CRO & Merch: increase conversion and average order value through catalog structure, comparisons, bundles, and site clarity

2. Current Position & Market Context

2.1. Market Trends 2026 (in simple terms)

  • Buyers increasingly choose energy efficiency and comfort (quiet, even temperature, savings)
  • Market transition to new refrigerants and "future-proof" products (R454B as main growth driver)
  • Growth of multi-zone as "whole-home comfort" solution
  • AI search: some informational queries are answered by AI, so citation and content structure are important

2.2. Competitive Environment

Competitors are strong in backlinks/DR and specific categories, but often weaker in brand consistency, AI visibility, and assortment depth. Our bet is assortment + trust + speed + clarity of choice.

3. Strategic Goals 2026

3.1. Business Goals (what should grow)

  • Revenue: seasonal growth focusing on April–July with margin control at peak
  • AOV (average order value): growth through good/better/best structure, bundles, multi-zone focus, and smart upselling
  • Email/CRM: increase channel contribution from current level to sustainable revenue share (through automation)
  • Organic: grow SEO traffic and AI answer share that leads to us (GEO)

3.2. Brand Goals (what should change in customer's mind)

  • MiniSplitsForLess is "the best place to buy mini split online" (recognition + trust)
  • Customer understands: we have wide selection, fast shipping, transparent delivery/returns, and support available
  • Brand looks consistent across all channels (visual style + tone of communication)

4. Channel Strategy (detailed)

This section covers the brief logic of each channel and then concrete action plans (what we do), so departments can turn them into tasks.

4.1. Google Ads Ecosystem (Search + PMax + Demand Gen + YouTube)

Channel role: primary sales driver for hot demand + brand support through video.

What We Do (core)

  • Search: split campaigns by intent (Brand / Category / BTU / Zones / Series / Features)
  • PMax: segment by major product groups (Single-Zone, Multi-Zone, Hyper Heat, R454B, top series)
  • Demand Gen: mid-funnel creatives for warming and remarketing
  • YouTube/Shorts: top and mid-funnel, explanation and trust

What Search Structure Looks Like (clear to everyone)

  • Brand Defense: 100% branded traffic protection
  • Generic mini split / mini split ac: general demand
  • BTU clusters: 9–12K, 18–24K, 36K, 48–60K (priority — whole-home)
  • Zones: single vs multi, 2–6 zones
  • Series/Brands: Cooper & Hunter, Olivia, Bravo, Olmo, Armbridge
  • Features: Hyper Heat, R454B, SEER 22/24, WiFi, etc.

Deliverables & Tasks for Departments

  • PPC: campaign map + keyword list + negatives + seasonal budget rules
  • Design/Video: asset packs for PMax/DemandGen/YouTube (by theme) + hook variations every 10–14 days
  • Dev/Analytics: proper GA4/Ads tracking + conversions + checkout/speed audit
  • Merch: list of priority SKUs/series for promotion based on margin and availability

4.2. Meta Ads (Facebook/Instagram)

Channel role: demand creation, warming, remarketing, and brand amplification through visual storytelling.

  • 60% video / 40% static (reels + carousels)
  • Pillars: comfort, quiet, savings, fast shipping, trust (reviews), clear choice
  • Structure: Prospecting → Warm (video/site) → Bottom-funnel (product/cart)
  • Creative rotation: new versions every 2–3 weeks; update hook more frequently

4.3. TikTok

Channel role: experiments and awareness growth. Not "direct hard-sell", but demand and memorability.

  • Formats: short explanations, comparisons, "expectation/reality", light HVAC humor
  • Series approach: repeating segments (so people recognize the format)
  • Best organic content → amplify with Spark Ads

4.4. Email Marketing & CRM

Channel role: LTV growth, returns, repeat purchases, converting "thinkers". No spam: fewer mass emails, more automation.

  • Core automations: Welcome, Browse Abandon, Cart Abandon (3 steps), Post-Purchase, Win-back, Review request
  • Mass campaigns: "Deals of the Month" once a month + 2–3 emails for real events/sales, twice a week
  • Segments: by interest (zones/BTU/series), by behavior (view/cart), by geography (hot vs cold states), by customer value
  • Frequency limits: per-person cap (to avoid burning the list)

4.5. SEO + GEO + AI Overviews

Role: stable non-brand traffic growth and citation in AI answers. We create content that AI can easily "pick up" and cite.

Key AI/Answer Engine Platforms (2026 target)

  • Google AI Overviews / Gemini (AI answers in search)
  • Bing AI + Microsoft Copilot
  • Perplexity (with citations)
  • ChatGPT (web answers with sources, where available)
  • Brave Search AI, Duck.ai, Yahoo AI, You.com, and other AI searches

Content System (what we publish)

  • Hubs: BTU sizing, zones, ducted vs ductless, cassette vs wall mount, Hyper Heat, refrigerants (R454B/R410A/R32)
  • Comparisons: brands and series (Cooper & Hunter vs Bravo, etc.)
  • Pages by capacity and zones: "9K for bedroom", "48K 5-zone whole-home", etc.
  • Tables and short "Answer Capsules": quick answer + table + links to selection

4.6. Social Media (Organic)

Role: awareness, trust, brand repetition. Organic supports paid channels and SEO.

  • Unified visual style (templates for Reels/Posts/Stories)
  • Regular sections: "how to choose", "selection mistakes", "series of the week", "Q&A", "customer case"
  • UGC and reviews: request content from customers and use in advertising (with permission)

5. Product & Merchandising Strategy

Goal: make assortment as "clear for choice" as possible and beneficial for ad scaling.

  • Good/better/best structure by series/brands (clear on collections and PDPs)
  • Priority on multi-zone and whole-home solutions in peak seasons
  • Filters and navigation: BTU, zones, unit type, refrigerant, voltage, SEER, series, brand
  • Bundles: complete offerings (system + compatible accessories), where business logic supports it
  • Merchandising rules for advertising: priority SKU list, margin/availability stop-list

6. Creative & Content Strategy

Creative is the "engine" of scaling. System matters more than one-off banners.

Unified Tone and Image

  • Expert, respectful, without "advertising screaming"
  • Focus on comfort and choice confidence: quiet, economical, fast delivery, support available
  • Niche HVAC humor — targeted and appropriate

Rotation and Production

  • Static: refresh every 4–6 weeks (change entry: background/headline/accent)
  • Video: new variations every 2–3 weeks; update hook every 10–14 days
  • 1 idea = 3–5 editing versions (length, hook, CTA, visual rhythm)

8. Success Metrics & KPIs

To make it clear to everyone what "success" means, we establish measurable metrics.

  • Brand: branded search growth, direct traffic, branded CTR
  • Paid: ROAS/POAS, CAC, CVR, impression share on priority queries
  • SEO/GEO: non-brand traffic, top pages, AI citations, CTR on informational pages
  • Email: revenue share, flow revenue, unsubscribe/spam rate, engagement
  • CRO: speed, mobile checkout, device conversion, AOV
